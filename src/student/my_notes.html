Wave-particle duality is a fundamental concept in quantum mechanics. It suggests that all particles exhibit both particle and wave properties. An electron, for example, can be thought of as a discrete particle, but it also has a wavelength and can interfere with itself like a wave.

The Uncertainty Principle, formulated by Heisenberg, states that it's impossible to simultaneously know both the exact position and the exact momentum of a particle. The more precisely one is known, the less precisely the other can be known.

Remember the first law of thermodynamics: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another. This is the principle of conservation of energy.
